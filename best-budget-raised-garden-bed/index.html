Raised garden beds are one of the easiest ways to start a vegetable or flower garden, especially if your soil is poor, you have back pain from bending, or you just want a tidy growing area. Budget models can be just as effective as expensive ones, but the material, size, and assembly method make the biggest difference in how long the bed lasts and how much work it is to set up. For a budget-friendly raised bed, look for rot-resistant wood (cedar or fir) or powder-coated metal that won’t rust quickly. A depth of at least 10–12 inches gives most roots enough room, and beds that are 4 feet wide or less let you reach the center easily. Avoid anything with flimsy corners or too-thin walls that might bow under soil weight.
